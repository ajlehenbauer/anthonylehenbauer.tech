If you want to know more about me:

I've been programming for about 7 years now, developing games for the last 3. I, ofcourse, play many games as well. Pokemon and Halo are series that I grew up with and will always view fondly, but ever since I started Stardew Valley, indie games take up most of my playtime now. Typically ones that are easy to pick up, and can play for shorter sessions that I inevitably end up playing for hours at a time. Aside from games, I spent my 4 and a half years in college working in the AV industry. I gained a good amount of knowledge surrounding AV production of live events, and I genuinely loved both being able to learn all of that equipment and work with so many different groups to make cool events happen.

Cross Analysis is easy to pick up, but difficult to master. Simply swap tiles so that each row and column has its highest number in the center position and is in decreasing order further from the center.
Random boards provide good casual play, the challenges give a chance to truly test your puzzle solving skills. I originally made this for Goole Sheets, but rebuilt it in Unity to expand on the core gameplay. The game is still in development, but this is a pretty good prototype!
